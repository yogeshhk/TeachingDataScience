\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{What Is AI?, A Business Leader's Definition}

\textbf{Artificial Intelligence:} Machines performing tasks that normally require human intelligence, perceiving, reasoning, deciding, creating.

\vspace{0.5em}
\textbf{The key shift from traditional software:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Traditional software: A programmer writes every rule explicitly.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textit{``If customer buys X, show offer Y''}, a human wrote that rule.
  \end{itemize}
  \item AI / Machine Learning: The system \textit{learns the rules from data automatically}.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textit{``We showed 10 million customers their history, the model figured out what to recommend.''}
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.3em}
\textbf{Plain English:} AI is a junior analyst who has read everything, never sleeps, and gets better every time it gets feedback, but still needs a senior leader to set direction and review critical decisions.

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{The AI Hierarchy, How the Pieces Fit}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{AI (broadest):} Any machine mimicking human intelligence
  \item \textbf{Machine Learning (ML):} AI that learns from data, no explicit rules needed
  \item \textbf{Deep Learning:} ML using neural networks; powers image recognition, speech
  \item \textbf{Generative AI:} Deep Learning that \textit{creates} new content
  \item \textbf{LLMs (Large Language Models):} The engine behind ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, trained on the internet at massive scale
  \item \textbf{AI Agents:} LLMs given tools (web, email, databases) to take autonomous actions
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.3em}
\textbf{For business leaders:} You don't need to build these. You need to understand which layer solves your problem, and which vendor or team to trust to build it for you.

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{Myths vs.\ Reality, What Every Leader Must Know}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Myth:} AI understands like a human does.\\
    \textbf{Reality:} AI identifies statistical patterns. It does not ``think.'' It can be confidently wrong.

  \item \textbf{Myth:} AI is always accurate.\\
    \textbf{Reality:} Models ``hallucinate'', generating plausible but incorrect outputs. Human oversight is essential for high-stakes decisions.

  \item \textbf{Myth:} AI will replace our workforce.\\
    \textbf{Reality:} AI replaces repetitive tasks, not roles. Employees who use AI well become 2$\times$--10$\times$ more productive. Demand for skilled people rises.

  \item \textbf{Myth:} We need to train our own AI model.\\
    \textbf{Reality:} For 95\% of business use cases, you \textit{integrate} existing AI (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) via APIs on top of your own data.

  \item \textbf{Myth:} Enterprise AI and consumer AI (ChatGPT free) are the same.\\
    \textbf{Reality:} Enterprise AI has data isolation, compliance, SLAs, cost controls, consumer tools do not.
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{AI What It Cannot Do, Honest Expectations}

Setting realistic expectations prevents failed projects and board-level disappointment:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{AI cannot reason causally:} It finds correlations, not true cause-and-effect.\\
    \textit{``Ice cream sales and drownings are correlated, the AI doesn't know summer is the cause.''}
  \item \textbf{AI cannot replace human judgment in ethical decisions:} Hiring, promotion, loan approval, legal and moral accountability stays with humans.
  \item \textbf{AI cannot work without data:} Garbage data in, garbage output out. Data quality is a prerequisite.
  \item \textbf{AI cannot explain itself easily:} Most powerful models are ``black boxes.'' Regulated industries (banking, healthcare) need explainability.
  \item \textbf{AI needs maintenance:} Models drift over time as the world changes. Ongoing investment required.
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{Prompting Patterns Every Leader Should Know}

You do not need to code. You need to communicate with AI effectively.
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Role Prompting:} ``You are a strategy consultant with 20 years in consumer electronics. Analyze this market data and identify the top 3 growth opportunities.''\\
    \textit{Why it works: Activates the right vocabulary, framing, and level of analysis.}

  \item \textbf{Context + Task:} Always give AI: Who you are, what you need, the format you want, and any constraints.\\
    \textit{``Write a 5-bullet executive summary of this report for a board audience with no AI background.''}

  \item \textbf{Iterate, Don't Accept First Output:} AI output is a first draft, not a final answer. Refine with follow-up prompts.\\
    \textit{``Make it more concise'' / ``Add a risk section'' / ``Change tone to be more assertive.''}

  \item \textbf{Structured Output:} Ask AI for tables, bullet points, or specific sections, output becomes more actionable.
\end{itemize}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{How to Think About AI ROI}

\textbf{The basic ROI question:} For every rupee invested in AI, what do I get back?

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Efficiency gains:} Time saved $\times$ volume $\times$ cost rate\\
    \textit{Example: AI triage reduces ticket handling from 15 min to 3 min. 1000 tickets/day = 200 hrs saved/day.}
  \item \textbf{Quality gains:} Errors avoided $\times$ cost per error\\
    \textit{Example: AI catches 80\% of compliance exceptions before they become regulatory fines.}
  \item \textbf{Revenue acceleration:} Faster product launches, better conversion, higher retention\\
    \textit{Example: Personalized marketing AI improves email CTR by 25\%.}
  \item \textbf{Cost avoidance:} AI handles Tier-1 support = fewer support hires needed
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Start with a baseline:} Measure your current state \textit{before} AI deployment. You cannot prove ROI without a before-and-after comparison.

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{AI Cost Components, Know What You Are Paying For}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Model / API Cost:} Pay-per-use pricing from AI providers (OpenAI, Google, Anthropic). Typically charged per 1,000 words processed.
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textit{Rule of thumb: Advanced AI $\approx$ USD 2--10 per million words. Fast, smaller AI $\approx$ USD 0.10--0.50 per million words.}
    \end{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Integration / Development Cost:} Engineering effort to connect AI to your systems (CRM, ERP, support tools)
  \item \textbf{Data Preparation:} Cleaning and structuring your existing data so AI can use it reliably
  \item \textbf{Infrastructure:} Cloud storage, vector databases, hosting
  \item \textbf{Security \& Compliance:} Data encryption, access controls, audit logging
  \item \textbf{Human Oversight:} Reviewers, governance team, ongoing quality monitoring
  \item \textbf{Training \& Change Management:} Staff adoption programs, policy documentation
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{FinOps for AI, Controlling AI Costs at Scale}

As AI adoption grows, costs can spiral without governance. Key controls:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Right-size the model:} Use the cheapest model that meets your quality bar. Do not use the most powerful AI for every task.
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textit{Simple task (summarize email): Use fast, cheap model. Complex analysis: Use advanced model.}
    \end{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Monitor cost per use case:} Track AI spend by department, by feature, by user.
  \item \textbf{Set budgets and alerts:} Treat AI API spend like cloud compute, set monthly budgets, get alerts at 80\% threshold.
  \item \textbf{Batch non-urgent tasks:} Run reports, analyses, and document processing overnight in bulk at 50\% lower cost.
  \item \textbf{Usage governance:} Define who can use which tier of AI. Require justification for expensive AI usage.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Target:} Define a \textit{cost-per-outcome} KPI (e.g., cost per resolved ticket, cost per generated lead) before launching any AI feature.

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{AI Governance, What Every Leader Must Establish}

Without governance, AI creates risk. With it, AI creates value safely.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{AI Usage Policy:} What data can go to AI tools? What is prohibited? (PII, trade secrets, financials never go into consumer AI tools.)
  \item \textbf{Approval Workflow:} How does a team get approval to deploy an AI feature to customers or operations?
  \item \textbf{Human-in-the-Loop Rules:} Which AI outputs require human review before action? (Customer communications, financial decisions, HR outcomes, always.)
  \item \textbf{Audit Trail:} Are AI interactions logged? Who can access the logs? For how long?
  \item \textbf{Incident Response:} What happens when AI makes a wrong decision that affects a customer or a regulatory requirement?
  \item \textbf{Vendor Review:} Are AI providers contractually bound not to use your data for training? Do they have the right certifications?
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{Key AI Risks, The Executive Risk Register}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Hallucinations:} AI confidently states wrong facts. Risk in customer communications, financial reports, legal documents.\\
    \textit{Mitigation: Ground AI with your own verified data (RAG); require human sign-off on high-stakes outputs.}
  \item \textbf{Data Leakage:} Employee pastes customer PII or trade secrets into a consumer AI tool.\\
    \textit{Mitigation: Enterprise-only AI policy; DLP tools; training.}
  \item \textbf{Bias \& Unfairness:} AI trained on historical data perpetuates historical inequities in hiring, pricing, lending.\\
    \textit{Mitigation: Regular bias audits; diverse data sources; human review of high-impact decisions.}
  \item \textbf{Regulatory Exposure:} EU AI Act (2024) classifies AI by risk level, high-risk use cases (HR, credit, healthcare) require conformity assessment.
  \item \textbf{Reputational Risk:} AI-generated content going public without review causing brand damage.\\
    \textit{Mitigation: Mandatory human approval gate for all external AI-generated content.}
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{AI Bias, Ethics, and Environmental Responsibility}

\textbf{Real-world bias examples every leader should know:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Amazon's hiring AI (2018) systematically downgraded female candidates, trained on male-dominated historical data. Shut down.
  \item Facial recognition tools showed significantly higher error rates for people with darker skin tones, led to wrongful arrests.
  \item Loan approval algorithms replicating historical ``redlining'' patterns, regulatory fines followed.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{What responsible leaders do:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Audit AI decisions regularly for disparate impact across groups
  \item Ensure diverse training data and diverse AI review teams
  \item Follow the EU AI Act risk-based classification framework
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Environmental note:} Training large AI models generates hundreds of tonnes of CO$_2$. AI also optimizes energy systems, net impact depends on how we use it. ``Innovation with responsibility.''

\end{frame}

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{AI Regulation Landscape, What Leaders Must Track}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{EU AI Act (2024, world's first comprehensive AI law):}
    \begin{itemize}
      \item Risk-based: Unacceptable risk (banned) $\rightarrow$ High risk (regulated) $\rightarrow$ Limited / Minimal risk
      \item High-risk categories include: HR / recruitment AI, credit scoring AI, critical infrastructure
      \item Mandatory conformity assessments, human oversight, transparency obligations
    \end{itemize}
  \item \textbf{US Executive Order on AI (2023):} Safety standards, red-teaming requirements for frontier AI models
  \item \textbf{India's approach:} Currently innovation-first, with MEITY guidelines evolving, expect regulation within 2--3 years
  \item \textbf{GDPR (Europe):} Any AI using EU citizen personal data must have legal basis and right-to-explanation provisions
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Leader action:} Have Legal and Compliance review AI deployments before launch. Build auditability in from day one.

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{Identifying AI Opportunities, The 4-Lens Framework}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Goal:} Spot functional challenges that AI can address.
  \item Apply the \textbf{4-Lens Framework} to every business problem:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{Efficiency:} Where are we spending time on tasks AI could do?\\
        \textit{Examples: Report generation, data extraction, document review}
      \item \textbf{Experience:} Where are customers or employees frustrated?\\
        \textit{Examples: Long support wait times, manual onboarding, repetitive queries}
      \item \textbf{Decision-making:} Where do we have data but lack clear insights?\\
        \textit{Examples: Pricing decisions, demand forecasting, risk assessment}
      \item \textbf{Innovation:} What new products or services become possible with AI?\\
        \textit{Examples: Personalized product lines, AI-powered advisory services}
    \end{itemize}
  \item Group brainstorming by function (Sales, Ops, HR, Finance, Marketing, Supply Chain)
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{AI Use Case Canvas, Template}

For each opportunity, fill in:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Problem Statement:} (1 sentence, what is broken or slow today?)
  \item \textbf{Current Pain:} (Quantify, hours/week, error rate, customer satisfaction score, cost)
  \item \textbf{AI-Assisted Future State:} What does the workflow look like with AI? Who does what differently?
  \item \textbf{AI Approach:} Existing SaaS tool? LLM API on internal data? Custom model?
  \item \textbf{Data Needed:} What existing data (documents, transactions, emails, records) does the AI need access to?
  \item \textbf{Success Metric:} How will you measure success? (e.g., 30\% reduction in processing time, NPS +10)
  \item \textbf{Risk / Constraint:} Data privacy concern? Regulatory requirement? Human review needed?
  \item \textbf{Effort Estimate:} Quick win (weeks) / medium-term (months) / strategic bet (6--12 months)?
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{Evaluating Feasibility and Impact}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Classify opportunities by implementation timeline:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{Short-term (0--3 months):} Existing AI tools, minimal integration, immediate productivity gains
      \item \textbf{Medium-term (3--9 months):} Custom AI on internal data, process redesign required
      \item \textbf{Long-term (9--18 months):} AI-native products, significant organizational change
    \end{itemize}
  \item Score each opportunity on:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{Business Impact:} Revenue, cost, experience (High / Medium / Low)
      \item \textbf{Implementation Effort:} Data availability, technical complexity, change management (High / Medium / Low)
      \item \textbf{Risk Level:} Regulatory, reputational, operational (High / Medium / Low)
    \end{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Prioritize:} High Impact + Low Effort + Low Risk = Start here
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{How to Drive AI Adoption, The Leadership Playbook}

\textbf{What kills AI adoption:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Random experimentation without shared learning, 20 teams, 20 tools, no coordination
  \item Fear and resistance, employees worried AI threatens their jobs
  \item No clear ownership, everyone waits, no one acts
\end{itemize}

\textbf{What works:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{AI Champions:} 1--2 enthusiastic adopters per team. Give them time, budget, and air cover.
  \item \textbf{Visible leadership use:} When the CEO and leadership team visibly use AI tools, adoption accelerates across the org.
  \item \textbf{Psychological safety:} Reward experimentation, not just success. Celebrate learning from failed AI pilots.
  \item \textbf{Shared learning:} Monthly AI wins newsletter / internal community. What worked, what didn't, how to replicate.
  \item \textbf{Quick wins first:} Show business impact within 30 days. Momentum is everything.
\end{itemize}

\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{Limits on AI, Staying Grounded}

Setting honest expectations prevents failed projects and board-level disappointment:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{No true reasoning:} AI finds patterns, not cause-and-effect. Human judgment remains essential.
  \item \textbf{Accuracy limits:} Every AI system has an error rate. Critical decisions need human review.
  \item \textbf{Data dependency:} AI is only as good as the data it is trained or grounded on. Data quality is a prerequisite, not an afterthought.
  \item \textbf{Black-box risk:} Most powerful AI models cannot explain their reasoning, regulatory and trust risk in high-stakes domains.
  \item \textbf{Maintenance burden:} AI systems drift over time as the world changes. Ongoing investment in monitoring and updates is required.
  \item \textbf{Change management:} Technology is the easy part. People, process, and culture change is the hard part.
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}
